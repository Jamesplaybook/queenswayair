Heat pumps have become the hottest topic in Ontario HVAC — and for good reason. A modern air-source heat pump can heat and cool your home for a fraction of the cost of traditional electric baseboards, while dramatically reducing your carbon footprint.

The federal and provincial governments are actively paying homeowners to make the switch. Here's a complete guide to the rebates available in 2026.

## How Much Can You Get Back?

Combining the two main programs, eligible Ontario homeowners can receive up to **$10,600** toward an air-source heat pump installation:

| Program | Maximum Rebate |
|---|---|
| Canada Greener Homes Grant | $5,000 |
| Enbridge Home Efficiency Rebate Plus (HER+) | $5,600 |
| **Combined total** | **$10,600** |

Not every homeowner qualifies for both programs, and rebate amounts vary based on the equipment you choose and your home's baseline energy rating. But even a partial rebate significantly reduces the cost of upgrading.

## Program 1: Canada Greener Homes Grant

The federal Greener Homes Grant provides up to $5,000 for the installation of qualifying air-source or ground-source heat pumps.

**Who qualifies:**
- Canadian homeowner (not a renter)
- Home built before 2023
- Unit must be an Energy Star certified heat pump meeting minimum efficiency requirements
- Pre-retrofit EnerGuide home evaluation required before installation

**How to apply:**
1. Register at [Natural Resources Canada's Greener Homes portal](https://www.nrcan.gc.ca/energy-efficiency/homes/canada-greener-homes-grant/23441)
2. Book a pre-retrofit EnerGuide evaluation with a licensed energy advisor
3. Have your heat pump installed by a licensed contractor (Queensway qualifies)
4. Book a post-retrofit EnerGuide evaluation
5. Submit your claim online with receipts

The process takes 2–4 months from application to payment. We help coordinate the evaluations and paperwork.

## Program 2: Enbridge Home Efficiency Rebate Plus (HER+)

The Enbridge HER+ program provides rebates of up to $5,600 for Enbridge Gas customers who install qualifying heating and cooling improvements, including heat pumps.

**Who qualifies:**
- Must be an Enbridge Gas residential customer
- Home must be in Enbridge's service area (covers most of the GTA, including Mississauga)
- Qualifying equipment must meet minimum HSPF2/SEER2 efficiency ratings

**How to apply:**
Apply directly through Enbridge's portal after installation. Queensway provides all required documentation at no charge.

## What About the Canada Greener Homes Loan?

Separate from the grant, the Canada Greener Homes Loan provides up to **$40,000 at 0% interest** with a 10-year repayment term. This can be combined with the grant and used alongside the Enbridge HER+ rebate.

If you're financing a new heat pump installation, the loan is worth exploring — it's interest-free government financing at terms better than any private lender.

## Do Cold-Climate Heat Pumps Work in Ontario Winters?

Yes. Modern cold-climate heat pumps (also called hyper-heat or ultra-low ambient units) operate efficiently in temperatures down to -30°C. Most Ontario homeowners pair them with their existing gas furnace in a "dual fuel" configuration — the heat pump handles mild to moderately cold days, and the gas furnace kicks in only during the coldest periods.

This hybrid setup maximizes comfort, minimizes operating costs, and makes the heat pump eligible for all rebate programs.
